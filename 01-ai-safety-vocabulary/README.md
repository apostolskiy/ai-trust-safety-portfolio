# AI Safety Vocabulary

This document is a working glossary of key terms related to AI Trust & Safety, Responsible AI, LLM evaluation, synthetic media risk, and AI red teaming.

## Terms

### AI Trust & Safety

The practice of identifying, reducing, and managing risks related to AI systems, user harm, platform abuse, unsafe outputs, and misuse.

### Responsible AI

An approach to designing, deploying, and evaluating AI systems in a way that considers fairness, transparency, accountability, safety, and human impact.

### LLM Evaluation

The process of testing and assessing large language model outputs according to specific criteria such as safety, helpfulness, factuality, clarity, and appropriateness.

### AI Red Teaming

A structured process of testing AI systems with challenging, adversarial, or unusual inputs in order to identify vulnerabilities, unsafe behavior, or misuse risks.

### Synthetic Media

Media generated or modified by AI, including images, video, audio, text, avatars, fake personas, and manipulated visual content.

### Misinformation

False or misleading information that may cause users to develop incorrect beliefs or make harmful decisions.

### Hallucination

A model output that sounds confident but contains false, unsupported, or fabricated information.

### Bias

A pattern in AI outputs that may unfairly favor, disadvantage, stereotype, or misrepresent certain people, groups, or perspectives.

### Unsafe Output

An AI-generated response that may enable harm, provide dangerous instructions, encourage misuse, or create risk for users or others.

### Edge Case

An unusual, ambiguous, extreme, or less common scenario used to test how reliably a system behaves outside normal conditions.
